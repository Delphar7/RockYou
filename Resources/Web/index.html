RockYou

A Roku remote that also controls your TV’s power & volume.

The official Roku app is great at controlling your Roku streamer. RockYou’s superpower is controlling both: the Roku for navigation/playback and the attached TV for power/volume — from one place.

Pairing: streamer + TV as one “setup”
D‑pad: swipe + tap (fast)
Safety: hold-to-confirm buttons
Watch: quick control companion
Household: share pairings & app MRU

Quick start

  1. Discover your Roku devices on Wi‑Fi.
  2. Pair a Roku streamer with the TV it’s connected to.
  3. Control: navigation goes to the streamer; power/volume go to the TV.
Why pairing matters: many streamers can’t control TV hardware directly. Pairing lets RockYou route the right command to the right device every time.

What you’ll notice

  • Fast navigation with a swipeable D‑pad.
  • Optional “hold to confirm” for sensitive actions.
  • App strip with your most recently used apps.

D‑pad + stick

Use the center as your anchor: tap for OK, swipe/drag for directions. It’s designed to be fast and forgiving.

  • Tap arrows for single presses.
  • Tap center for OK.
  • Drag from center for repeated navigation.

Sweepable buttons (hold-to-confirm)

Some actions are easy to hit by accident (power, home, launching apps with a delay). RockYou can require a short hold, with a clear progress ring.

  • Tap when a button is configured for instant action.
  • Hold to confirm when configured for safety.
  • Tooltips explain what a hold does if you release early.

Watch companion

The watch is for quick control: navigation + OK + volume. Perfect for “pause/mute/skip” without pulling out your phone.

  • Fast pages for common actions.
  • Volume on crown (where it belongs).
  • Uses your paired setup from the phone.

Household sharing

RockYou can share your household’s paired devices and “most recent apps” ordering, so everyone sees the same setups across devices.

  • Share pairings (TV + streamer).
  • Share MRU apps per device, across the household.
  • Works across iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Watch companion.

Tips & troubleshooting

  • Discovery: make sure phone and Roku are on the same Wi‑Fi.
  • Limited Mode: some Rokus block mobile control. Enable it in Roku settings if needed.
  • Multiple rooms: name your devices clearly (e.g. “Living Room TV”, “Bedroom Ultra”).
